About

I am currently pursuing a B.S. in Computer Engineering and Computer Science at USC. Since January of 2018 I’ve worked as a research intern at Sandia National Laboratories. My research involves applications of machine-learning techniques in different fields from network intrusion detection to new methods for anomaly detection in time series data.

Moving forward, I am interested in theoretical research on applying machine learning to improve quantum computing hardware and control of quantum information systems. More generally, I am interested in the following fields:

  • Quantum Computing / Quantum Information
  • Machine Learning
  • Computational Physics
  • Hardware Acceleration
